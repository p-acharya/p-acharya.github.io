I am a PhD student in Computer Science at George Mason University, where I study computational models of language acquisition and cognition. My research focuses on human-grounded models of learning and processing, especially where second language acquisition, multilingual NLP, and model evaluation meet.

Before doctoral study, I worked as a software engineer on large-scale data platforms in industrial manufacturing and healthcare. That experience continues to shape how I think about language technology: not only as a scientific object, but as a system that must be interpretable, robust, and useful in practice.

I have also worked on low-resource machine translation, interpretable multilingual NLP, and language-related tools across several languages and scripts. My broader goal is to build computational approaches to language that connect scientific explanation with practical value.
