### Computational Psycholinguistics, Language Acquisition, and NLP

My research spans learner language, human sentence processing, multilingual NLP, and cognitively grounded evaluation.

## Introduction

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at George Mason University. I work at the intersection of NLP, cognitive science, and second language acquisition, with a focus on how language is learned, processed, and generalized across humans and models. My work combines computational modeling, behavioral evaluation, corpus design, and applied technical systems.

## Research Agenda

My work is organized around a set of connected questions about language, learning, and model behavior.

### Language acquisition and learner variation
How do learners acquire structure, and how does a speaker's first language shape second-language production over time?

### Human-grounded model evaluation
When do language models align with human behavior, and when does apparent success obscure deeper limits in representation, processing, or generalization?

### Computational models of language processing
What kinds of probabilistic or neural models best account for observed patterns in human reading and comprehension?

### Cross-linguistic and multilingual language technology
How can insights from linguistic structure and human learning inform better multilingual NLP systems and language-related tools?

## Selected Work

### [Tracing L1 interference in English learner writing](/projects/#tracing-l1-interference-in-english-learner-writing)
A longitudinal corpus with error annotations for studying how native language shapes learner English, and for evaluating whether models capture transfer effects in principled ways.

### [Controlled generation for language pedagogy](/projects/#controlled-generation-for-language-pedagogy)
Research at ETH Zurich on controllable text generation for pedagogy, expressing lesson goals as explicit constraints and evaluating both linguistic correctness and pedagogical fit.

### [Human-grounded modeling across languages](/research/)
Current doctoral work on computational predictors of language learning and processing, linking model-based measures to behavioral data across languages.

### [Production-grade data systems](/projects/#large-scale-data-systems-and-infrastructure)
Prior engineering work building and maintaining large-scale data platforms, distributed pipelines, and real-time systems in industrial and healthcare settings.

## Research and Applied Collaborations

I am interested in collaborations that connect language learning, model evaluation, multilingual NLP, and cognitively grounded AI. My work is especially relevant to learner modeling, educational AI, language assessment, cross-linguistic analysis, and human-centered language technology.
