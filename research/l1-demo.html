word-for-word mapping · Mandarin → English

You probably understood this fine. There is nothing wrong with the grammar. But the phrasing is just slightly awkward. Why did the writer choose these exact words?

The phrasing is awkward in English because it is not English. It is a Mandarin construction, translated word for word.

What a standard rewrite misses

A standard LLM would silently rewrite this as “in terms of learning.”, with no account of why it was written this way.

That rewrite erases the cognitive footprint entirely. Detecting what’s actually happening requires fluency in Chinese and English, deep linguistic knowledge, and SLA pedagogy.

This project automates that exact expertise, extracting thousands of these structural fingerprints across a longitudinal corpus of 620 essays.
